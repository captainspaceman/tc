App Store Responses

Itunes and Google Play are popular forums for TrueCar customers to voice their concerns.

Being mindful of the old adage "the customer is always right" comes in handy when communicating with TrueCar customers in these two forums. Follow these tenets of civil correspondence when dealing with any customer concerns.

Listen First

You can't help a customer through their problem unless you know what that problem is. Read through their entire complaint, then reach out to them.

Always be sure that you understand the issue a user is facing before you make any claims or offer any instructions.

Practice Empathy

Customer service copywriting is all about practicing empathy.

Always make a genuine effort to let the customer know that you care, and that you are taking their concerns and complaints seriously.

Never Argue

The easiest way to turn a customer service engagement sour is to become angry or defensive.

The most important thing to keep in mind is to always try and see things from the customer's point of view. If a customer is complaining about TrueCar in a public forum, then the chances are very good that they are already irritated to begin with.

If you get angry, they'll get more angry. And then nobody wins.

Don't Repeat Yourself

Prepared reponses to customer concerns can be very useful if you're dealing with a common issue.

However, sometimes this can lead to robot-like copywriting where you're saying the same thing over and over. Not ideal.

Send them to CS

It's best not to drag out a conversation on social media or in a public forum like the App store or Google Play. The most effective way to solve a customer service issue is to direct it to our excellent customer service department (888) 878-3227.

Message the number to the user and ask if they can give us a call.
